Hospital Resource Optimization and Demand Forecasting System

An AI-powered healthcare decision support platform for predicting demand, optimizing hospital resources, and improving proactive planning.

Hospitals often face critical challenges such as overcrowded wards, inefficient scheduling, medicine shortages, and delayed responses to disease outbreaks. Most existing systems operate in a reactive manner, addressing problems only after they occur.

This research introduces an AI-powered Hospital Resource Optimization and Demand Forecasting System that enables proactive decision-making. By combining machine learning models, time-series forecasting, optimization techniques, and explainable AI, the system predicts future hospital demands and provides actionable insights for better planning.

The system integrates multiple components including patient flow prediction, staff scheduling optimization, medicine demand forecasting, and illness trend detection. This helps hospitals improve efficiency, reduce delays, and enhance patient care through data-driven decision-making.

Research Domain

Literature Survey

Previous research in hospital systems has explored several areas such as patient scheduling, medicine demand prediction, and disease forecasting. Scheduling systems have focused on reducing waiting time using optimization techniques. Medicine demand forecasting studies have used statistical and machine learning models to predict usage patterns.

Bed demand forecasting research has used time-series models to estimate patient admissions. Disease trend forecasting studies have analyzed environmental and historical data to predict outbreaks. However, most of these systems operate independently and do not provide an integrated solution.

Research Gap

Most existing systems focus on solving individual hospital problems separately, such as scheduling, bed allocation, or disease prediction. There is a lack of a unified system that integrates all these components together.

Additionally, many systems lack real-time adaptability and do not provide explainable outputs, making it difficult for healthcare professionals to trust AI-based decisions.

Research Problem

Hospital resource management is currently reactive and inefficient. This results in overcrowded wards, underutilized resources, poor scheduling, and medicine shortages.

There is a need for an intelligent system that can predict future demand, optimize resources, and provide clear decision support to healthcare staff.

Research Objectives

  • Predict patient demand using historical and real-time data
  • Optimize hospital bed allocation and utilization
  • Improve doctor and staff scheduling efficiency
  • Forecast medicine demand and emergency requirements
  • Detect illness trends and potential outbreaks early
  • Provide explainable AI outputs for better decision-making
